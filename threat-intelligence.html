Manufacturing Sector Threat Landscape

72
Incidents (April 2025)
-21%
Change from March
Qilin
Primary Threat Actor

Intellectual Property Targeting

Manufacturing firms are experiencing targeted attacks focused on stealing intellectual property and design specifications before deploying ransomware, creating dual extortion scenarios.

OT/IT Convergence Risks

Threat actors are exploiting the increasing connectivity between operational technology (OT) and information technology (IT) networks, with a focus on legacy systems with unpatched vulnerabilities.

Supply Chain Compromise

Attackers are targeting smaller suppliers and vendors in the manufacturing supply chain as entry points to larger organizations, leveraging trusted relationships to move laterally.

Recommended Security Controls

  • Implement network segmentation between IT and OT environments
  • Deploy enhanced monitoring for intellectual property and design files
  • Conduct regular vulnerability assessments of industrial control systems
  • Establish vendor security assessment program for supply chain partners
  • Develop and test specific incident response playbooks for manufacturing attacks

Lazarus
Primary Threat Actor

SWIFT Network Targeting

Financial institutions are experiencing sophisticated attacks targeting SWIFT messaging systems with malware designed to manipulate transaction records and bypass authentication.

API-Based Attacks

Attackers are exploiting vulnerabilities in banking APIs to initiate unauthorized transactions, with a focus on mobile banking applications and payment gateways.

Supply Chain Compromise

Third-party financial software providers are being targeted as entry points to multiple banks and financial institutions through supply chain attacks.

Recommended Security Controls

  • Implement transaction verification systems with multi-person approval
  • Deploy API security gateways with strict rate limiting
  • Conduct regular red team exercises focusing on payment systems
  • Enhance monitoring for anomalous SWIFT message patterns
  • Establish vendor security assessment program for fintech partners

Patient Data Targeting

Healthcare organizations are experiencing targeted attacks focused on stealing protected health information (PHI) for medical identity theft and insurance fraud.

Medical Device Vulnerabilities

Connected medical devices with outdated firmware are being exploited as entry points to hospital networks, particularly infusion pumps and imaging systems.

Ransomware Impacting Care Delivery

Ransomware attacks are increasingly disrupting patient care, with threat actors specifically targeting systems critical to emergency services and surgical operations.

Recommended Security Controls

  • Implement network segmentation for medical IoT devices
  • Deploy enhanced monitoring for PHI access patterns
  • Conduct regular vulnerability assessments of medical devices
  • Establish incident response playbooks for clinical system outages
  • Develop backup procedures for critical patient care systems

APT41
Primary Threat Actor

Software Supply Chain Attacks

Technology companies are being targeted through their software development pipelines, with attackers compromising build systems to distribute malware to customers.

Cloud Infrastructure Targeting

Attackers are exploiting misconfigured cloud services and weak identity management to gain access to sensitive customer data and proprietary source code.

Zero-Day Exploitation

Technology firms are experiencing a surge in zero-day attacks targeting their products, with vulnerabilities being exploited before patches can be developed.

Recommended Security Controls

  • Implement software bill of materials (SBOM) for all products
  • Enforce multi-factor authentication for all cloud services
  • Conduct regular red team exercises focusing on CI/CD pipelines
  • Deploy runtime application self-protection (RASP) for critical services
  • Establish bug bounty programs to identify vulnerabilities
