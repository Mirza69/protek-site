Cyber Threat Categories

Our security experts have categorized the most prevalent threats facing UK businesses today by severity and attack vector.

Critical Threat

Ransomware Attacks

Ransomware remains the most disruptive cyber threat to UK businesses, with attackers increasingly targeting critical infrastructure and supply chains. Modern ransomware strains now incorporate data theft and extortion tactics.

Endpoint Protection Backup Strategy Email Filtering Patch Management

Recent UK Attack Trends

  • Double extortion tactics (encryption + data theft)
  • Targeting of cloud backups and recovery systems
  • Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) proliferation

Evolution of Ransomware

2017-2019: Widespread Encryption

Simple encryption attacks with payment demands in cryptocurrency

2020-2021: Double Extortion

Attackers began stealing data before encryption to pressure victims

2022-Present: Triple Extortion

Added DDoS attacks and contacting customers/business partners

Notable UK Incidents

2024: NHS Supply Chain Attack

Disrupted medical equipment deliveries to 45 hospitals for 3 weeks

2023: Royal Mail Disruption

LockBit ransomware halted international shipments for 5 days

2022: South Staffordshire Water

Attackers gained SCADA access but were stopped before contamination

High Threat

Phishing & Social Engineering

Phishing remains the most common initial attack vector, with increasingly sophisticated tactics targeting employees at all levels. Business Email Compromise (BEC) scams cost UK companies millions annually.

412% increase in QR code phishing in 2024
Average BEC loss: £45,000 per incident

Current Phishing Trends

  • AI-generated personalized phishing emails
  • QR code phishing ("quishing") bypassing email filters
  • Voice phishing (vishing) targeting finance departments

Email Phishing

Deceptive emails impersonating trusted entities to steal credentials or deliver malware

Vishing

Phone calls impersonating IT support or bank officials to extract sensitive information

Smishing

Text messages with malicious links or requests for personal/financial information

High Threat

Supply Chain Compromises

Attackers increasingly target software vendors and service providers to gain access to multiple organizations through a single breach. These attacks can bypass traditional security controls by exploiting trusted relationships.

78% increase in supply chain attacks since 2022
Average dwell time before detection: 287 days

Recent UK Supply Chain Incidents

  • 2024: Accounting software compromise affected 200+ SMEs
  • 2023: Managed service provider breach led to ransomware across clients
  • 2022: Third-party logistics provider attack disrupted retail inventory

Supply Chain Attack Vectors

Software Updates

Compromised software updates delivering malware to all users

Cloud Services

Breach of shared cloud infrastructure or SaaS providers

Third-Party Access

Exploiting vendor remote access or privileged accounts

High Threat

Cloud Security Threats

As UK businesses accelerate cloud adoption, misconfigurations and identity management failures create new attack surfaces. Attackers exploit these weaknesses to access sensitive data and deploy malicious workloads.

81% of organizations experienced a cloud security incident in 2024
Overprivileged accounts present in 92% of cloud environments

Top Cloud Threats

  • Misconfigured storage buckets exposing sensitive data
  • Compromised credentials and API keys
  • Malicious container images in cloud registries

AWS-Specific Threats

  • IAM privilege escalation vulnerabilities
  • Public EC2 instances with critical ports open
  • Unprotected S3 buckets containing sensitive data

Azure-Specific Threats

  • Overprivileged service principals
  • Unsecured storage accounts with anonymous access
  • Misconfigured conditional access policies

Emerging Threats

Our threat intelligence team monitors developing attack vectors that may impact UK businesses in the near future.

AI-Powered Attacks

Attackers leveraging generative AI to create highly personalized phishing content, mimic voices for vishing, and automate target reconnaissance at scale.

Expected to increase 300% by 2026

5G Network Exploits

As 5G adoption grows, vulnerabilities in network slicing, edge computing, and IoT device connectivity create new attack surfaces for threat actors.

Critical infrastructure at particular risk

Quantum Computing Threats

Future quantum computers may break current encryption standards. While not imminent, "harvest now, decrypt later" attacks are already a concern.

Threat Protection FAQs

How can I tell if my business is being targeted by these threats?

▼

Common indicators include unusual network activity, failed login attempts from unfamiliar locations, unexpected system behavior, and reports from employees about suspicious communications. Our threat detection services can monitor for these signs 24/7 and alert you to potential threats before they cause damage.

What's the most effective defense against ransomware?

▼

A multi-layered approach is essential: maintain offline backups, implement endpoint detection and response (EDR) solutions, restrict user permissions, keep systems patched, and conduct regular security awareness training. Our ransomware protection service combines all these elements with 24/7 monitoring.

How often should we reassess our threat landscape?

▼

We recommend quarterly threat assessments for most businesses, with monthly reviews for high-risk industries. The cyber threat landscape evolves rapidly, and our threat intelligence subscriptions provide continuous updates about new risks relevant to your sector.

Are SMEs really at risk or just large enterprises?

▼

SMEs are frequently targeted precisely because they often have weaker defenses than large enterprises. 60% of small businesses close within 6 months of a significant cyber attack. Our SME-focused security packages provide enterprise-grade protection at scales and prices appropriate for smaller organizations.

What should we do if we suspect we've been breached?

▼

Immediately contact our incident response team (available 24/7). Don't turn off affected systems (this destroys forensic evidence), but disconnect them from the network if safe to do so. Avoid interacting with attackers until consulting professionals. We'll guide you through containment, eradication, recovery, and reporting steps.
